Research

Research Interests

I am a interdisciplinary explorer. My research interests are utilizing the power of machine learning and remote sensing technologies to tackle pressing global challenges, especially within the agricultural and environmental sectors. I am more focused on the novel implementation of machine learning (ML) and deep learning (DL) methodologies in these fields, as I strongly believe in crafting domain-specific models that meet reliability, generalizability, and novelty rather than straightforward implementations.

At present, I am actively exploring some core research questions related to the sustainable environment, precision agriculture, energy, and power systems. By exploiting my expertise in machine learning and deep learning, I strive to develop innovative solutions that not only promote sustainability but also deepen our understanding of complex systems and their interconnections.

Additionally, I am trying to center around the practice of explainable AI in the field of sustainability. I am also highly interested in scientific machine learning, such as physics-informed neural networks (PINNs), as this has promising results in different complex domains.

I am not restricted to a particular field of sustainability. Therefore, my interests broadly include:

  • Machine Learning and Remote Sensing in Agriculture & Environment

  • Sustainable Agriculture & Environment

  • Smart Agriculture

  • Data-Driven Novel Approaches

  • Scientific Machine Learning

  • Hydrology

  • Water Resource Engineering

  • Hydrology

  • Energy and Power Systems

  • Land Use and Climate Change

  • Bioenergy

  • Interdisciplinary Research

  • Fundamental Machine Learning

Collaboration

I am passionate about collaborative research and currently have the privilege of working with esteemed researchers from Cornell University, Columbia University in The City of New York, and the University of Science and Technology of China. This collaboration allows me to engage with diverse perspectives and expertise, enriching my research and contributing to impactful outcomes.
